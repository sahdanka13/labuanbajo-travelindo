TOP 1

Padar (Komodo Island)

A visit to Labuan Bajo wouldn't be complete if you haven't been to Komodo Island. This is where animals in the form of ancient lizards live and roam. Recognized as a world heritage site by UNESCO and one of the seven wonders of the natural world from the New 7 Wonders of Nature, Komodo Island is a must-see spot in Labuan Bajo. The exoticism of the endemic animal Komodo is increasingly enriched by its enchanting natural landscape. The natural beauty of this island stretches out from the lush trees, green hills and charming beaches. It'll make you forget to go home!

TOP 2

Pink Beach

From the name alone it is clear that this beach has a close relationship with the color pink. Well, Pink Beach Labuan Bajo is a beach with pink sand. Not black or white like most beaches. The uniqueness of Pink Beach is that it is only one of seven beaches with pink sand in the world. Where does the pink color come from? Investigating further, it turns out that the color is caused by coral fragments and marine life which are actually pink. Have you imagined an instagrammable photo with the unique background of this beach? Don't miss the underwater beauty of Pink Beach because its charm is no less charming. You can also do snorkeling and diving if you vacation here.

TOP 3

Kanawa Beach

The next tourist spot in Labuan Bajo is Kanawa Island. The Kanawa Island area is one of the favorite destinations for tourists in Labuan Bajo. The underwater life really attracts tourists who enjoy swimming and diving. Arriving at Kanawa Island, the expanse of white sand and clear blue sea water immediately welcomed you warmly. It is so clear, the underwater beauty can be clearly seen from the boat. Various types of coral reefs and other marine biota can be seen in the waters of Kanawa Island. If you're not good at diving, relaxing and enjoying the beach atmosphere by playing in the sand and sunbathing is still an exciting activity.
